Structural Essay Questions (SEQ)

1. A 48 years industrial worker presented to you with history of severe breathlessness for 3 days. Examination reveled wide spread rhonchi on auscultation of lungs.

Total: 10 Marks
a. What are the provisional diagnosis of this case. 2 Marks

The provisional diagnosis is Zeeq-un-Nafas Shabi, caused by the accumulation of morbid, viscid phlegm (Balgham-e-Ghaleez) in the bronchioles. Clinically, this correlates to the modern diagnosis of Acute Exacerbation of Bronchial Asthma (specifically Occupational Asthma given the industrial history).

b. How will you confirm your diagnosis? 3 Marks
  • Unani Assessment: Assessment of Nabz (Pulse) and observation of sputum characteristics (thick, viscid, difficult to expectorate).
  • Modern Diagnostics:
    • Pulmonary Function Tests (Spirometry) showing reversible airflow obstruction.
    • Peak Expiratory Flow Rate (PEFR).
    • Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) to rule out severe hypoxemia/hypercapnia.
c. Write down the elaj bil dawoa wa geza of this case. 3 Marks

Usool-e-Elaj: Immediate avoidance of occupational triggers. First, Nuzj (concoction) of thick phlegm, followed by Tanqia-e-Mawwad using Mushilat-e-Balgham. Finally, Taqwiyat-e-Riya (strengthening the lungs) and Tawsi-e-Shuab (bronchodilation).

Elaj bil Dawoa (Pharmacotherapy):

  • Unani Medicines: Sharbat-e-Zufa Murakkab (10-20 ml BD in warm water), Habbe Zeeq-un-Nafas (1-2 pills BD), Laooq-e-Sapistan (10g BD).
  • Modern Integration: Prescribe short-acting bronchodilators (Salbutamol) via inhaler using proper spacer technique for immediate relief of bronchospasm.

Elaj bil Geza (Dietotherapy):

  • Har Mizaj (hot temperament) foods. Lukewarm water with honey. Strict avoidance of Aghziya Barida wa Ratba (cold & wet foods like yogurt, cold water) that produce excess Balgham.
d. Pen down the pachidgiya (complications) of this case. 2 Marks
  • Status Asthmaticus.
  • Respiratory Failure.
  • Cor Pulmonale (secondary to chronic pulmonary hypertension).

2. A 40 years male come to hospital with history of asteshca(ascites) and recurrent right hypochondriac pain with azmol a kabed(hepatomegaly).

Total: 10 Marks
a. What is your provisional diagnosis 2 Marks

The provisional diagnosis is Istisqa-e-Ziqqi occurring as a direct complication of Zoaf-e-Kabid and Su-e-Mizaj Kabid Barid. Clinically, this correlates with the modern diagnosis of Liver Cirrhosis with Ascites secondary to Portal Hypertension.

b. Pen down the asbaab (aetiology) of this case. 2 Marks
  • Unani Asbaab: Buroodat (coldness) overpowering the liver's innate heat (Hararat-e-Ghariziya). This weakens the liver's Quwwat-e-Jaziba (absorptive) and Quwwat-e-Dafia (expulsive) faculties, leading to pooling of Maddah-e-Ma'iya (watery fluid) in the peritoneum.
  • Modern Causes: Chronic alcoholism, Chronic Viral Hepatitis (HBV/HCV), Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD), and Autoimmune Hepatitis.
c. How will you confirm your diagnosis? 3 Marks
  • Ascitic Fluid Analysis: SAAG (Serum Ascites Albumin Gradient) ≥ 1.1 g/dL confirms portal hypertension etiology.
  • USG Abdomen: Shrunken nodular liver with ascites.
  • LFTs: Hypoalbuminemia, deranged liver enzymes (AST/ALT ratio).
d. Write down the usool a elaj and elaj of this case. 3 Marks

Usool-e-Elaj: Restore Hararat-e-Ghariziya (innate heat) using Musakhkhin-e-Kabid (warming) drugs. Evacuate accumulated fluid via Idrar-e-Baul (diuresis) and Tariq (diaphoresis). Prevent further hepatic damage.

Elaj (Treatment):

  • Unani Dawoa: Sharbat Bazoori Motadil (20ml BD), Arq Mako & Arq Kasni (60ml mixed BD), Majoon Dabeed-ul-Ward (5g BD).
  • Modern Integration: Spironolactone (aldosterone antagonist) +/- Furosemide. Therapeutic Paracentesis if tense ascites causes dyspnea.
  • Diet: Strict Sodium restriction (< 2g/day).

3. A 45 years male patient presented low graded fever with multiple joint pain, morning stiffness and skyline ESR.

Total: 10 Marks
a. What are the provisional diagnosis of this case. 2 Marks

The provisional diagnosis is Waja-ul-Mafasil Balghami. The clinical presentation of symmetrical polyarthritis and morning stiffness > 1 hour strongly correlates with the modern diagnosis of Rheumatoid Arthritis.

b. How will you confirm your diagnosis? 3 Marks
  • Serology: Rheumatoid Factor (RA Factor) and Anti-CCP antibodies (Anti-CCP is highly specific for RA).
  • Inflammatory Markers: ESR (already elevated) and CRP.
  • Radiology: X-ray of joints showing bony erosions and joint space narrowing.
c. Write down the elaj bil dawoa wa geza of this case. 3 Marks

Usool-e-Elaj: Tanqia-e-Mawwad (evacuating the morbid *Balgham*) followed by Muhallil-e-Waram (anti-inflammatories) and Musakkin-e-Alam (analgesics). Normalize the Mizaj.

Elaj bil Dawoa:

  • Unani Medicines: Majoon Suranjan (5g BD), Habbe Asgand (2 pills BD).
  • Ilaj bil Tadbeer: Local Zimad or Dalak (massage) with Roghan Suranjan.
  • Modern Integration: Early initiation of DMARDs (e.g., Methotrexate) is essential to prevent joint destruction.

Elaj bil Geza: Avoid Aghziya Barida (cold/damp foods like rice, curd). Promote ginger, garlic, and turmeric.

d. Pen down the pachidgia (complications) of this case. 2 Marks
  • Joint deformities (Swan-neck, Boutonnière deformities).
  • Felty's Syndrome (Triad of RA, splenomegaly, and neutropenia).
  • Atlanto-axial subluxation (dangerous cervical spine instability).
  • Rheumatoid nodules, vasculitis, and secondary Amyloidosis (long-term).

Short Answer Questions (SAQ)

4. What are the alamat(symptoms) and elaj of Kabah (Depression). 2+3 Marks

Alamat:

  • Persistent Huzn (sadness), anhedonia, fatigue, and insomnia.
  • Qillat-e-Ishtaha (loss of appetite) and psychomotor retardation.
  • Fikr-e-Fasid (morbid/irrational thoughts) and in severe cases, self-destructive tendencies.

Usool-e-Elaj: Kabah is caused by Ghalba-e-Sawda (dominance of black bile) shifting the brain's Mizaj to Barid Yabis (Cold & Dry). Treatment demands Tanqia-e-Sawda and Taadil-e-Mizaj using Mufarrihat (exhilarants).

Elaj (Treatment):

  • Unani Medicines: Khamira Gaozaban Ambari Jawahar Wala (5g OD morning), Itrifal Ustukhuddus (5g at bedtime for *Tanqia*).
  • Modern Integration: SSRIs (e.g., Fluoxetine) and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for severe cases.
5. Pen down the symptoms and elaj of sue-mijaj meda ratab-gayer maddi. 5 Marks

(Note: Abnormal wet dyscrasia of the stomach without physical accumulation of matter).

Symptoms:

  • Sluggish digestion (Zofe-Hazm), Ghathayan (nausea), and Takhammur (belching).
  • Excessive salivation, a white-coated tongue, and feeling of fullness after taking minor meals.
  • Pale urine and a distinct lack of thirst (Qillat-e-Atash).

Usool-e-Elaj: Restore stomach heat and absorb abnormal moisture using drugs and diets of Har Yabis (Hot and Dry) temperament. As it is *Gayer Maddi* (non-material), no *Tanqia* (evacuation) is required, only qualitative correction.

Elaj & Geza:

  • Medicines: Jawarish Kamuni (5g PC / after meals), Jawarish Mastagi.
  • Diet: Dry, roasted foods. Avoid excess fluid intake during or immediately after meals.
6. Pen down the alamat wa awarejat and dietary suppliment of hypokalemia. 2+3 Marks

Alamat: Hypokalemia (Serum K⁺ < 3.5 mEq/L) correlates with Zoaf-e-Asab and Zoaf-e-Uzlati. Manifests as muscle weakness, cramps, paralytic ileus, and palpitations. ECG changes: prominent U waves, flattened T waves, ST depression.

Note: Normal Serum Potassium (K⁺) range is 3.5 - 5.0 mEq/L.

Management:

  • Dietary Supplements: Bananas, coconut water, oranges, and spinach.
  • Medical Treatment (Crucial): Mild cases: Oral KCl syrup. Severe cases (< 2.5 mEq/L): Slow IV KCl infusion under continuous ECG monitoring.
7a. A patient 40 years presented with Yarkan. How will you consider clinically what type of Yarkan is it? 3 Marks

Differentiating Yarkan (Jaundice):

  1. Pre-hepatic (Hemolytic): Lemon-yellow tint. Normal urine/stool. Associated with anemia.
  2. Hepatic (Hepatocellular): Orange-yellow tint. Dark urine, normal stool. Anorexia, tender liver.
  3. Post-hepatic (Obstructive): Greenish-yellow skin. Very dark urine (Bilirubinuria), pale/clay-colored stool. Severe pruritus and biliary colic.
7b. Write down the elaj(treatment) of Yarkan. 2 Marks

Usool-e-Elaj: Focus on Tasfiya-e-Dam (blood purification), resolving hepatic inflammation, and flushing out excess Safra (yellow bile) through urine using Mudir-e-Baul (diuretics).

  • Unani Medicines: Arq Kasni & Arq Mako (60ml mixed BD), Sharbat Bazoori Motadil (20ml BD), Majoon Dabeed-ul-Ward.
  • Diet: Sugarcane juice, glucose water, strictly fat-free diet.
8a. A 50 years old male presented with profuse bleeding per mouth. What are the possibilities? Enumerate the causes of GIT haemorrage? 2+2 Marks

Possibilities: Hematemesis (Upper GIT) or Hemoptysis (Respiratory). Context implies Upper GIT bleed.

Causes of Upper GIT Haemorrhage:

  1. Peptic Ulcer Disease (Gastric/Duodenal).
  2. Esophageal Varices (due to portal hypertension/cirrhosis).
  3. Mallory-Weiss tears (mucosal tears from severe vomiting).
  4. Acute erosive gastritis / gastric erosions.
  5. Angiodysplasia (vascular malformations).
  6. Aortoenteric fistula.

Modern Mgt: Immediate IV fluids, cross-match blood, IV PPI (Pantoprazole), and urgent Endoscopy.

8b. Write down the asbaab and elaj bil dawoa wa geza of Bawoasir (Hemorroids). 3 Marks

Unani Asbaab: Accumulation of Mawwad-e-Sawdawi (melancholic humors) in the anal veins, usually secondary to chronic constipation (Qabz).

Usool-e-Elaj: Relieve constipation using Mulayyanat (laxatives). If bleeding, use Habis-e-Dam (hemostatics).

  • Dawoa: Habbe Bawasir Khuni (2 pills BD), Majoon Muqil (5g BD). Local Zimad of Marham Bawasir.
  • Geza: High-fiber diet and plenty of fluids.
9. Write down the normal range of following report. 5 Marks
  • a. S. Creatinine: 0.6 - 1.2 mg/dL
  • b. TSH: 0.4 - 4.0 mIU/L
  • c. ALT (SGPT): 7 - 56 U/L
  • d. S. Cholesterol: < 200 mg/dL
  • e. RBS: 70 - 140 mg/dL
10. Write down the predisposing factors and diagnostic criteria of Tadarrune Revi (Pulmonary Tuberculosis). 2+3 Marks

Predisposing Factors & Clinical: Unani literature links it to Zoaf-e-Riya (lung weakness) leading to Sil (wasting) and Daq (hectic fever). Modern predisposing factors include overcrowding, malnutrition, and immunocompromised states (especially HIV/AIDS). Clinical buzzwords: Significant weight loss, chronic cough > 2 weeks, evening rise of temperature, and night sweats.

Diagnostic Criteria:

  • Microbiology: Sputum smear for AFB.
  • Molecular: CBNAAT (GeneXpert) for rapid MTB detection and Rifampicin resistance.
  • Immunology: Mantoux Test (Tuberculin Skin Test) to show TB exposure.
  • Radiology: Chest X-ray PA view showing upper lobe cavitations/infiltrates.
11. Write down the alamat(symptoms) and elaj bil dawoa wa geza of anal fissure. 5 Marks

Alamat (Shiqaq-ul-Miqad): Severe tearing pain during defecation, streaks of bright red blood on stool, and severe sphincter spasm.

Usool-e-Elaj: Eradicate constipation, heal the mucosal tear, and relieve anal sphincter spasm.

  • Unani Medical: Use Mulayyanat like Itrifal Mulayyan (10g at bedtime with warm water) or Roghan Badam Shirin. Use Zimad-e-Muzliq (lubricating ointments) like Marham Ral locally. Warm sitz bath.
  • Modern Conservative: Chemical sphincterotomy using Topical GTN (0.2%) ointment or Diltiazem cream to relax the sphincter. Botox injections for refractory cases.
  • Modern Surgical: If conservative measures fail, Lateral Internal Sphincterotomy (LIS) is required.
  • Geza: High fluid and fiber intake.
12. A 40 years female come to hospital with history of vomiting and recurrent abdominal pain following a fatty meal which is radiated to right shoulder. Diagnosis and management? 5 Marks

Clinical Diagnosis: Waram-e-Keesa-e-Safra due to Hasa (gallstones), which clinically correlates to Acute Cholecystitis / Biliary Colic. The history perfectly matches the "4 Fs" risk factors (Female, Forty, Fat/Fatty food trigger).

Confirmation: USG Abdomen to detect gallstones and gallbladder wall thickening.

Management:

  • Conservative: NPO, IV fluids, IV Analgesics/Antispasmodics, IV Antibiotics.
  • Definitive: Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy.
13a. Write down the difference between IHD and MI. 3 Marks

Unani Context: Both correlate broadly with Zeeq-un-Nafas Qalbi due to Suddah (obstruction) in Urooq-e-Qalb.

Feature IHD (Stable Angina) MI (Myocardial Infarction)
Pathology Ischemia (inadequate blood flow) due to narrowing. Necrosis (tissue death) due to complete blockage.
Damage Temporary & Reversible. Permanent & Irreversible.
Pain Relief Relieved by rest or Sublingual Nitroglycerin. Not relieved by rest; requires IV opioids (Morphine).
Cardiac Markers Normal. Elevated (Troponin, CK-MB).
13b. Pen down the pachidgiya (complications) of IHD. 2 Marks
  • Acute Myocardial Infarction.
  • Cardiogenic Shock.
  • Cardiac Arrhythmias (Ventricular Fibrillation).
  • Congestive Heart Failure.
14. What information you will find out from urine R/E. 5 Marks

In Unani medicine (Tafseel-e-Qaroora), urine color (Lawn) and consistency (Qiwam) are primary indicators of dominant humors (Akhlat). Modern Urine R/E evaluates:

  • Physical: Color (dark yellow in jaundice/Safra dominance), Appearance (cloudy in UTI), Specific Gravity.
  • Chemical: Protein (renal damage), Glucose (Diabetes), Ketones, Bilirubin (liver pathology), and Occult Blood.
  • Microscopic: RBCs (hematuria), Pus cells/WBCs (infection), Crystals, and Casts:
    • RBC casts → Glomerulonephritis.
    • WBC casts → Pyelonephritis.
    • Granular casts → Tubular damage / ATN.
